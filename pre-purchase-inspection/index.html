[01] Why it matters

The cost of skipping it.

Termite damage costs Australian property owners an estimated $1.5 billion a year, and a home can look flawless on a walk-through while carrying an active or long-settled infestation behind the walls. Without a written inspection, a buyer has no leverage to negotiate and no warning before settlement — just a hope that everything's fine.

Hidden until it's expensive

Subterranean termites and borers work behind plaster, under floors and inside roof framing. A property can present perfectly at an open home while structural timber underneath tells a very different story.

No negotiating room

Findings from a pre-purchase inspection can be used to request repairs or renegotiate price before you're locked in. Skip it, and any damage discovered after settlement is entirely yours to fix.

Certainty either way

A clean report is worth as much as one that flags an issue — either way, you're signing with an accurate picture of the property rather than an assumption.

[03] Standard & scope

AS 4349.3 compliant.
Termidor accredited.

Our pre-purchase inspections follow Australian Standard AS 4349.3 for timber pest inspections — a non-invasive, visual inspection covering subterranean and dampwood termites, borers of seasoned timber, and wood decay fungi. Furniture isn't moved and walls aren't cut into; the standard is deliberately built around what's safely accessible on the day.

In scope — subterranean and dampwood termites, borers of seasoned timber, and wood decay (rot), plus conditions on the property likely to attract them.
Out of scope — drywood termites, mould and rodents fall outside AS 4349.3 and would need a separate specialist assessment if suspected.
Pairs with a building inspection — AS 4349.3 covers timber pests only. Most buyers pair this report with a separate AS 4349.1 structural building inspection from a licensed building inspector for full coverage.
