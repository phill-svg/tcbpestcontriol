[01] Species

Three species. Three different responses.

The right response starts with the right identification. European wasps, paper wasps and honey bees each behave differently around the home — and the treatment, timing and equipment for one species will be wrong for the others.

European Wasps

The Canberra species worth knowing by name. Bright yellow and black, narrow waist, smooth body — and they nest underground in lawn, compost heaps, retaining-wall cavities and old animal burrows. Disturb the nest and you'll get a rapid, sustained attack from many guards at once.

  • Annual nests can hold several thousand workers
  • Multiple entrances — never block one yourself
  • Same-day dispatch during wasp season

Paper Wasps

Open, umbrella-shaped nests under eaves, on fence tops and inside meter boxes. Less aggressive than the European wasp, but still capable of multiple painful stings when the nest is disturbed — especially during the late-summer colony peak.

  • Open comb nests — visible from ground level
  • Treatable during daylight hours
  • Preventive spray around eaves and pergolas

Honey Bees & Native Bees

European honey bees and our native pollinators are essential to the local environment — and wherever we can safely refer a swarm or hive to a registered beekeeper, we will. We step in only when the swarm is genuinely a risk and there's no relocation option.

  • Swarm referral to local beekeepers first
  • Treatment only when relocation isn't viable
  • Documented identification on every visit

[02] Process

Three steps from sighting
to sealed site.

Same documented process on every wasp and bee visit — clear identification, full protective-gear treatment, and a scheduled follow-up inside our guarantee window.

Identification

On-site identification of the species and the nest location, including estimated colony size and the agitation level on the day. Photos and notes go into your written report so the right treatment is matched to the right risk.

Treatment

Targeted application in full protective gear, including respirator, gauntlets and bee suit. For active nests near play areas, schools or commercial premises we offer after-hours and weekend dispatch so families and staff are clear during the work.

Prevention

After removal we seal the nest site where possible — filling old burrows, screening roof-void and meter-box gaps, treating eaves as required. A scheduled follow-up inside the guarantee window confirms the colony is gone.

Full protective equipment

Every wasp and bee visit is carried out in a full bee suit with respirator, gauntlets and eye protection. We never treat without it — and we never ask a household to handle an active nest before our arrival.
