[01] The species

Two species. Two different plans.

Every visit starts with species ID — the plan that works for mice is wrong for rats. Treatment is built around what's actually there.

House Mice

Fast, fertile and quick to settle into subfloors and roof voids. Droppings in the pantry, gnaw marks on packaging and scratch-runs behind the fridge are the usual first signs.

Roof Rats

The climbing rat of Canberra roofs — through power lines and tree canopies, into the ceiling, then into the kitchen through gaps the size of a 50-cent piece.

[02] Process

Inspection, treatment,
exclusion. In writing.

The same three-step rhythm on every rodent job — what we found, what we did, and how we keep them out. Backed by our 100% satisfaction guarantee and a written report after every visit.

Inspection & entry-point survey

A full walk-through of the roof void, subfloor, perimeter and outbuildings. We map every entry point larger than a 10-cent piece, locate active runs, and identify the species before anything is set down.

Targeted baiting, trapping & proofing

Tamper-resistant bait stations and snap traps placed only along confirmed runs — never scattered, never in the open. Exclusion mesh installed where the entry survey calls for it.

Exclusion & follow-up

Steel-wool and mesh seals on every identified gap, written report with photos, and a scheduled revisit to check stations and confirm the colony is cleared. Re-treatment under our guarantee if activity returns.

[03] Long-term exclusion

Bait clears the colony.
Proofing keeps it out.

Bait and traps deal with what's there today — exclusion work is what stops the same problem returning next season. Every proofing visit ends with a photo-logged list of sealed entries and recommended building fixes, so you can either book the repair through us or hand the list straight to your builder.

[Safety] Humane, pet-safe, audit-ready

Safety is the first decision, not the last.

Humane methods are the default. Rodent bait is locked inside tamper-resistant stations, secured to fixed points and labelled in line with NSW EPA requirements. For food businesses, cafés, schools and child-care sites, bait placement is planned around your HACCP layout, your service hours and your bin store, with stations kept clear of food-prep, storage and play areas — every visit logged against a written station map.

[Pest focus] Mice & Rats

A rodent problem is rarely one mouse. We'll find the colony.

One mouse in the kitchen is rarely one mouse — Canberra roof voids and subfloors can hold dozens before you've seen a single one. We map the runs, clear the colony and seal the entry points they came in through.
