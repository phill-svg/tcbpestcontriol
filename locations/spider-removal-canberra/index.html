[02] Spider pressure

Where spiders hide
across Canberra.

Canberra's climate — cold winters, hot dry summers — concentrates spider activity into predictable seasonal peaks and habitat types. Understanding the pattern is half the job. Here's what our technicians see most often across the ACT.

Redback populations build from October through March in south-facing sheltered spots — sheds, letterboxes, outdoor chairs and garden structures.
White-tails move indoors from March as temperatures drop, entering through sub-floor vents, weep holes and gaps around pipes.
Black house spiders are active year-round, building persistent lacy webs on brick crevices, window frames and eaves across every Canberra suburb.
Funnel-webs are most prevalent in Belconnen, Weston Creek and Molonglo Valley — suburbs with direct access to bush reserves and scrub corridors.
Wolf spiders are disturbed by late-autumn leaf-clearing and garden work on larger Canberra blocks, appearing suddenly on patios and driveways.

[05] FAQ · Spider removal

Questions Canberra residents ask first.

Common questions about spider removal in Canberra — from species identification and seasonal timing to product safety and how long a treatment actually lasts.

Which Canberra spiders are medically significant?

Redbacks and funnel-webs are the two species to take seriously. Redback envenomation causes pain, nausea and local swelling — hospitalisation is uncommon but antivenom exists and should be sought. Funnel-webs are less frequently encountered in the ACT than in coastal NSW, but reserve-adjacent properties in Belconnen, Weston Creek and Molonglo Valley do see them. White-tails are commonly feared, but Australian toxicology research has largely disproven the necrosis myth — bites produce localised pain and irritation in most cases. We treat all of them regardless.

How often should I have spider treatment done in Canberra?

Most Canberra homes benefit from two treatments a year — one in September or October before summer spider populations build, and one in March or April as temperatures drop and spiders move indoors. Properties with large gardens, sub-floor access or direct reserve adjacency (Belconnen, Weston Creek, the Molonglo corridor) often need a third mid-summer visit to maintain the exterior barrier. We'll recommend a schedule based on your property type and what we find on the first visit.

Is the spider spray safe for children and pets?

Yes — we use family-safe insecticide formulations on every residential job. For the exterior barrier, normal access typically resumes 30–60 minutes after treatment once the product has dried. For any interior treatment we'll walk you through specific re-entry timing before we start. We use low-odour, low-residue products indoors and will tell you exactly what was applied and where.

How long does a spider treatment last?

An exterior perimeter barrier typically provides effective protection for three to six months under normal conditions — exposure to heavy rain, direct UV and foot traffic can shorten that. Interior treatments last longer because there's less environmental exposure. If you're seeing active spiders within 30 days of a treatment, call us — we'll return at no extra charge. Otherwise we recommend the twice-yearly schedule described above for ongoing protection.

Do you treat the inside of the house as well?

Yes, if the species warrants it. White-tails and redbacks occasionally establish indoors — we treat wall cavities, sub-floor spaces, roof voids and internal wall junctions where appropriate. For most other species (black house, wolf spiders) an exterior barrier is the primary tool because they don't regularly colonise interior spaces. We assess on the day and include interior treatment in the quote when it's needed.

Can I book spider removal as a standalone service, or does it need to be bundled?

Standalone spider removal is available as its own service across all Canberra and ACT suburbs. Many clients also bundle spider treatment with a general pest service (ants, cockroaches, silverfish) because the exterior spray covers multiple species in a single visit — often better value and more comprehensive. We'll quote both options so you can decide what makes sense for your property.
