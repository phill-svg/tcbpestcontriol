[02] Possum removal

Possum removal in the ACT — done legally.

The common brushtail possum is protected native wildlife in the ACT, and removing one without a licence is an offence. We trap and relocate possums humanely, then identify and seal every entry point to prevent the same animal, or another, from returning.

Inspection first

We walk the perimeter, roof void and subfloor to identify entry points and the current nest location. We tell you exactly what we found before any work starts — with a written assessment.

One-way exclusion

We install a one-way exit door on the primary entry point. The possum leaves at night to forage and can't return. This is the legal, humane standard under ACT wildlife regulations.

Seal all entry points

Once the possum is confirmed out, we seal every secondary entry — broken tiles, gap fascias, broken vents — so no new animal can move in. Written report provided on completion.

[03] Bird management

Bird noise, droppings and nest control.

Pigeons and starlings establish nest sites quickly and return each season if left undisturbed. We install spikes, netting and eave proofing to discourage birds from settling without disturbing surrounding native wildlife.

The damage birds cause

Bird droppings are mildly corrosive and cause lasting damage to roof surfaces, solar panels and gutters. Left unmanaged, a single nesting pair can expand into a persistent seasonal colony.

Humane exclusion methods

We install stainless-steel spikes, netting and eave proofing selected to the species and site. No poison, no lethal control — humane deterrents that protect your property without affecting surrounding native birds.

Stunned or injured birds

If a bird has hit a window and is stunned, place it gently in a dark, quiet box with ventilation holes and contact ACT Wildlife — the ACT's volunteer-run rescue service for injured and orphaned native animals across the region.

[04] Injured wildlife

What to do if you find an injured wild animal in Canberra.

For injured or orphaned native wildlife, the right call is ACT Wildlife or your nearest vet. A few animals require specialist handling before you make contact — here is what to know.

Wombats, kangaroos, echidnas, frogs, lizards and turtles

Call ACT Wildlife or take the animal to the nearest vet. If the animal has a joey in the pouch, mention this when you call so a trained volunteer can respond with the appropriate care.

Flying foxes and bats — specialist handling required

Do not handle flying foxes or bats without specialist training and equipment. Australian Bat Lyssavirus is a documented health risk to anyone bitten or scratched, according to the Australian Department of Health. Contact ACT Wildlife for a trained responder.

Dead wildlife on your property

For deceased wildlife on your property, contact us and we will arrange prompt removal. This applies to all native and feral species across Canberra and the wider ACT region.

ACT Wildlife — the right first call

ACT Wildlife is the ACT's volunteer-run rescue service for injured and orphaned native animals and birds across the region. For non-emergency situations, your nearest vet is the next step.
