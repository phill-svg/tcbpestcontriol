[01] How to know

The signs that point to mosquito or fly pressure.

Standing water nearby

Blocked gutters, pot-plant saucers, unused containers or poor drainage — any standing water is a potential mosquito breeding site within days.

Dusk and dawn bite pressure

Mosquito activity peaks at dusk and dawn — outdoor entertaining areas become unusable at exactly the times people want to use them.

Flies around bins and food areas

Persistent fly activity around bins, compost or food-prep areas points to a breeding or attraction source nearby, not just passing traffic.

Increased activity after rain

Both mosquitoes and flies spike after rain and warm weather — new standing water and faster organic breakdown both play a part.

[02] Process

Three steps to a usable outdoor space.

Mosquito and fly pressure is an outdoor-harbourage problem first — removing breeding sources does more than any single spray, and the perimeter treatment holds the line in between.

Harbourage inspection

We walk the property to identify standing water, breeding sites and fly-attraction points around bins and food areas.

Standing-water & source reduction

Practical fixes and screening for water sources that can't be removed outright — the single biggest lever for mosquito numbers.

Perimeter treatment

Residual barrier spray around outdoor entertaining areas, lawn edges and harbourage vegetation, reducing pressure between visits.

Commercial food-safety alignment

For commercial kitchens, fly-control measures and reporting can be adapted to your existing HACCP plan.

[03] Good to know

Outdoor treatments need seasonal reapplication — here's why.

Weather resets the barrier

Rain and irrigation break down outdoor residual treatments faster than indoor applications — reapplication through the warmer months is normal, not a sign the first visit failed.

Source reduction outperforms spraying alone

Removing or screening standing water does more to cut mosquito numbers long-term than spray treatment on its own.

A real seasonal health consideration

Mosquito-borne illness risk exists across the ACT region in warmer months — reducing bite exposure around the home is a genuine health measure, not just comfort.

Flies are a food-safety issue commercially

For hospitality and food premises, fly pressure is a compliance concern as much as a nuisance — we can align service timing and reporting with your food-safety plan.

[04] Prevention tips

What keeps mosquito and fly numbers down.

Empty standing water weekly

Pot-plant saucers, unused containers, clogged gutters — tip out anything holding water at least weekly during the warmer months.

Keep bins sealed and clean

A tight-fitting lid and a regular clean-out reduces the main fly-breeding and attraction source around most properties.

Maintain lawn and garden edges

Overgrown, shaded vegetation near entertaining areas is prime mosquito resting habitat during the day.
