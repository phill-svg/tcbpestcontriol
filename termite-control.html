[01] Signs

The signs of termite activity in a Canberra home.

Subterranean termites travel through concealed mud tunnels and rarely surface where you can see them. These four signs are the ones worth acting on — the earlier they're caught, the less structural damage there is to repair.

Mud tubes

Pencil-width mud tunnels running up foundations, piers, brick veneer or subfloor timber. This is how subterranean termites travel between the soil and the timber they're feeding on, sheltered from the open air.

Discarded wings & frass

Small piles of shed wings near windowsills after a warm-weather swarm, or fine frass (termite droppings) collecting near skirting boards and architraves.

Hollow-sounding timber

Skirting boards, architraves or structural framing that sound hollow or papery when tapped. Termites feed from the inside out, so the surface can look intact while the timber underneath is gone.

Sticking doors & blistered paint

Doors and windows that suddenly stick, or paint that blisters and gives slightly when pressed, can point to warping timber behind the surface — worth a professional check rather than a repaint.

[02] Why it matters

Why termites need a professional response.

Termites aren't a pest you spray and forget. A colony left untreated grows slowly at first, then compounds — and DIY sprays or surface treatments can scatter a colony deeper into the structure rather than dealing with it.

Hidden structural damage

Subterranean colonies move through plaster, masonry and concealed framing. A floorboard can look pristine while the joist beneath it is hollow — tapping, probing and moisture mapping reveal what a visual check alone will miss.

DIY sprays don't solve it

Off-the-shelf sprays kill visible workers but rarely reach the colony itself. The safest, most reliable response is a Termidor-accredited in-ground barrier and ongoing monitoring — not a can from the hardware store.

[03] Seasonal pressure

When termite activity picks up in Canberra.

Termite pressure in the ACT follows a seasonal pattern, driven by soil moisture and temperature. Autumn is the best window for a new barrier — installed before winter, ready to protect through the following spring swarm.

Spring

Warm, humid days after rain can trigger a swarm of winged alates — often the first visible sign a colony is nearby.

Summer

Peak underground activity as colonies forage widely for moisture and cellulose across the property.

Autumn

The ideal window for inspections and new in-ground barrier installation, ahead of the cooler months.

Winter

Activity continues below ground and inside heated structures, even though surface signs are less common.

[04] FAQ

The questions Canberra households ask first.

Honest answers from the same written reports we leave behind on every visit. Anything we can't answer here, we'll cover on the quote.

Are termites really a problem in Canberra?

Yes. Subterranean termites are well established across the ACT, and the region's clay soils and established gardens create conditions they thrive in. Most Canberra homes are within reach of an active colony somewhere nearby.

Can I treat termites myself?

We don't recommend it. Surface sprays rarely reach the colony and can scatter termites deeper into the structure, making the eventual professional treatment harder. An accredited in-ground barrier is the reliable fix.

How do I know if it's termites and not just old timber?

Mud tubes are the clearest giveaway — general timber decay doesn't produce them. If you're unsure, a professional inspection with tapping, probing and moisture mapping will confirm it either way.

What happens after termites are confirmed?

We move to treatment — a Termidor HE in-ground barrier around the affected area, with monitoring stations placed to track ongoing pressure. Full detail is on our termite treatment page.

Should I get an inspection before buying a home?

Yes — a pre-purchase timber pest inspection is the standard way to catch termite activity before settlement. See our termite inspection page for what's covered.

How much does termite treatment cost?

Each job is priced individually based on property size, construction and the extent of any activity found. We send a written quote before any work starts.
