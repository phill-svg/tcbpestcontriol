[01] Species

Common spider species in Canberra homes.

Spiders in Canberra are active year-round, with numbers peaking in late summer and autumn as spiders mature and males roam in search of mates. Australia is home to more than 2,000 species of spider — and while most pose little risk, several found in Canberra homes can deliver a painful or medically significant bite. These are the species we treat most regularly.

Redback spider

Found in dry, sheltered spots — under outdoor furniture, inside letterboxes, along retaining walls and in garden sheds. Redbacks account for more envenomations reported to Australian poison information centres than any other spider. The female's bite causes significant pain, sweating and nausea; antivenom has been available since 1956.

White tail spider

A roaming hunter, the white tail enters homes at night and is often found under bedding or inside clothing left on floors. Bites cause localised pain and redness. The long-held belief that white tail spider bites cause skin necrosis is not supported by scientific evidence.

Huntsman spider

Large and fast-moving, commonly found behind picture frames, under car sun visors and beneath loose bark on outdoor timber. Despite their size, huntsmans are not considered dangerous — their venom does not cause serious harm to healthy adults, and their instinct when disturbed is to flee rather than bite.

Wolf spider

A ground-dwelling hunter common in Canberra gardens and grassy areas. For identification help and specific treatment options, see our wolf spider Canberra page.

Black house spider

Builds dense, funnel-shaped webs in window frames, eaves and brickwork. Webs around windows and in corner joins are a reliable indicator of activity. Common throughout established Canberra suburbs year-round.

Mouse spider

A burrowing spider found in garden beds and lawns. The male mouse spider is considered potentially dangerous — its venom is similar in composition to that of funnel-web spiders and should be treated with the same caution. Any confirmed sighting warrants professional assessment.

Funnel-web spider

While the Sydney funnel-web is more prevalent north of the ACT, related funnel-web species occur across the region. Any suspected funnel-web sighting warrants careful avoidance and professional assessment — do not attempt DIY removal.

[ Dangerous spiders · Canberra ]

Two species that warrant genuine concern.

Most spiders found in Canberra homes pose little medical risk to healthy adults, but two species warrant genuine concern: the redback and the mouse spider. Redback spider bites are painful and, in children or older adults, can require antivenom and hospital care. If a person is bitten and symptoms worsen — spreading pain, sweating or muscle cramps — seek medical attention promptly and bring the spider for identification where it is safe to do so.

  • Redbacks account for more reported envenomations in Australia than any other spider species. Antivenom has been available since 1956 and treatment is effective when sought promptly.
  • The male mouse spider's venom is similar in composition to that of funnel-web spiders, according to the Australian Venom Research Unit at the University of Melbourne, and should be treated with the same caution.
  • Huntsman spiders cause alarm due to their size but are not dangerous in the medical sense. They produce venom not harmful to humans under normal circumstances, and a bite is uncommon unless the spider is directly handled or trapped against skin.

[03] Prevention

How to get rid of spiders between visits.

To get rid of spiders in your home and prevent them returning, remove the three things they need: access points, harbourage and food supply. These measures help prevent spiders between treatments, reducing re-entry and limiting spiders around the home. They work best alongside periodic expert spider control in Canberra, particularly before late summer.

Seal entry points

Seal gaps around pipes, cable penetrations and door frames with weather strip or flexible sealant. These are the primary structural entry routes for roaming species including white tails entering at night.

Repair fly screens

Repair or replace torn fly screens, particularly on ground-floor windows, which are the main entry route for common spider species entering at night from garden beds and perimeter zones.

Clear storage clutter

Clear clutter from garages and storage areas where spiders nest undisturbed for months at a time. Boxes, bags and equipment stored on floors create the dark, sheltered spots redbacks and black house spiders prefer.

Move garden debris

Move timber stacks, pot plants and garden debris at least 30 centimetres away from the building perimeter. Ground-level harbourage directly against the house gives burrowing and ground-dwelling spiders immediate access to the structure.

Yellow-spectrum lighting

Replace standard outdoor lights with yellow-spectrum bulbs. Yellow light attracts fewer insects, and fewer insects around your home means fewer hunting spiders following them to your walls and eaves at night.

Vacuum webs regularly

Vacuum webs and spiders around windows and in corner joins regularly. Removing webs and egg sacs disrupts the habitat rather than simply relocating individual spiders — and removes the visual cues that attract males during dispersal season in late summer and autumn.

[04] Product safety

APVMA-registered products. Safe for families and pets.

The insecticides our spider control experts use are registered with the Australian Pesticides and Veterinary Medicines Authority (APVMA), which requires all registered products to meet safety standards for human and animal health before approval. We follow label requirements on every job, including re-entry intervals.

Safe re-entry after drying

Treated surfaces are generally safe to re-enter once dry, which typically takes a few hours. We recommend keeping children and pets away from treated areas until surfaces have dried, and ventilating the property during and after any indoor application.

Full disclosure on every job

Wipe down kitchen surfaces after treatment as a precaution. As with any professional pest control visit, we tell you exactly which products were applied and answer any questions before we leave — every job closes with a written report.
