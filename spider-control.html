[01] Species

The spiders showing up around Canberra homes.

Most species that wander into a Canberra home are harmless. The four below are the ones we treat for most — either because they pose a real bite risk, or because their indoor appearances are uncomfortable enough that a careful exterior treatment is the right answer.

Sydney funnel-web

The dangerous Sydney funnel-web is established in and around the ACT. Burrows in moist lawn, mulch and garden beds. Active after rain and during warm evenings. Professional pest management — not a DIY job.

White-tail spider

The medium-sized white-tail often found indoors, particularly in bedrooms and laundry rooms. Bites can cause local pain and skin reaction.

Redback spider

The classic redback — under outdoor furniture, in the firewood stack, around sheds, fence tops and meter boxes. Distinct red stripe on a dark body.

Huntsman & orb weavers

Larger spiders that most Canberra households tolerate — but large indoor appearances are uncomfortable. Web-down and relocation-friendly treatment.

[02] Process

Four steps from call-out to a quieter home.

Every spider visit follows the same documented rhythm — inspect first, web-down the visible population, treat the harbourage at the perimeter, then close out with a typed report so you know exactly what was done. Backed by our 100% satisfaction guarantee.

Inspection

Walk the perimeter, the subfloor, garden beds, sheds and the wood pile. We map active web locations and identify harbourage areas — including any confirmed funnel-web burrows.

Web-down

Removal of visible webs and egg sacs from exterior surfaces, eaves, fences, sheds and outdoor furniture. Reduces the visible population immediately and disrupts the colony network.

Harbourage & perimeter treatment

Crack-and-crevice application along the foundation line, eaves, meter box surrounds and known burrow locations. Low-toxicity residual that lasts the season.

Written report

Every visit closes with a typed report — what we found, what we used, where attention was paid. Covered re-treatment inside our guarantee window if a treated species returns.

[03] Where we treat

Outside-in treatment, with inside monitoring.

Spider work is mostly a perimeter problem. We lead with exterior treatment — that is where the colony lives, and that is where activity stops. Inside the home, target only where the signs warrant it.

Exterior focus

Most spider treatments are perimeter-led — eaves, foundation line, meter box, subfloor vents, garden beds and known burrow locations. Crack-and-crevice application, low-odour, child-safe after the surface dries.

Interior only when required

Web-down and harbourage dusting for inside the home when activity warrants. Most treatments don't require interior application at all — but we always leave a typed report on what was done.

[04] Seasonal pressure

When spiders are most active in Canberra.

Canberra's spider season has a clear rhythm. Pre-winter exterior treatment catches the bulk of the year's pressure, with a spring refresh to take out any survivors that reappear after the cold.

Spring

Early outdoor activity picks up around September. Web-down preparation before the season takes hold.

Summer

Peak activity — most calls. Funnel-web sightings, redback density, white-tails inside.

Autumn

Indoor movement increases as outdoor temperatures drop. Pre-winter exterior treatment is the most effective timing.

Winter

Reduced outdoor activity but interior sightings remain common. Targeted inside treatment where required.

[05] FAQ

The questions Canberra households ask first.

Honest answers from the same written reports we leave behind on every visit. Anything we can't answer here, we'll cover on the quote.

Are funnel-webs really in Canberra?

Yes. Sydney funnel-webs are well established in the ACT, particularly around the Molonglo and Cotter corridors and the suburbs that back onto nature reserves. Treat any confirmed burrow as a medical-grade spider — call a professional, don't attempt DIY.

Is the treatment safe around pets and kids?

Yes. We use the lowest-toxicity option that does the job. Once surfaces dry, children and pets can return. Re-entry times are on every quote.

Will the spiders come back?

Inside the guarantee window — no. Long-term, exterior web-down twice a year, a barrier refresh ahead of autumn, and quick removal of firewood stacks near the house will keep most species under control.

How much does spider control cost in Canberra?

Each job is priced individually based on property size, species mix and pressure. We send a written quote before any work starts.

Do I need annual cover?

For most households, an annual or twice-yearly exterior treatment is enough.
