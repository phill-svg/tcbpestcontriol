[01] Treatment methods

How professional bed bug treatment works.

Bed bugs survive by hiding in cracks, seams and cavities where surface products can't reach. Professional treatment uses residual chemistry applied systematically to every harbourage point — followed by a second visit to catch any eggs that hatch after the first round.

Thorough inspection first

Before any product is applied, every sleeping area and soft-furnishing room is inspected systematically — mattress seams, bed frame joints, headboard cavities, bedside tables, skirting and behind wall fixtures. We confirm infestation level and identify all active harbourage zones before quoting treatment.

Targeted residual treatment

Low-toxicity residual insecticide is applied directly to all identified harbourage points — mattress seams, bed frame timbers and metal, headboard gaps, surrounding skirting and any other refuges found during inspection. Residual chemistry continues working for days to weeks after application, catching insects that weren't present during the visit.

Follow-up inside guarantee window

Bed bug eggs are resistant to chemical treatment. A second visit — typically two to three weeks after the initial treatment — catches the next generation before it can re-establish. This follow-up is included, not an add-on, and is written into your quote upfront.

[02] Every harbourage point

Where bed bugs actually live — and how we reach them.

Mattress & base

Mattress seams, piping, tufts and both sides of the base — the primary harbouring zone. We treat the full seam perimeter, not just the visible surface spots.

Bed frame & headboard

Joints, screw holes, hollow legs and the headboard cavity are high-density harbouring points. We treat every joint and accessible gap in the frame and headboard structure.

Bedside furniture & skirting

Bedside tables, skirting boards adjacent to the bed, the wall-floor junction and areas where cables run are inspected and treated wherever evidence is found.

Lounge & soft furnishings

Heavy infestations spread to lounges, recliners and upholstered chairs. Any room with soft furnishings where people sleep or rest is included in the inspection scope.

[03] Before the technician arrives

How to prepare
for bed bug treatment.

A small amount of preparation makes treatment significantly more effective. You don't need to bag every item in your home — but the areas around the bed and sleeping zones must be accessible. Your written quote includes specific preparation instructions for your property.

  • Strip and bag all bedding — wash on the highest heat the fabric tolerates
  • Clear the floor around the bed so the base and skirting are accessible
  • Leave luggage and travel bags in the room for inspection and treatment
  • Vacate the property for the re-entry time stated on your quote
  • Secure or relocate pets and cover fish tanks as per the technician's instructions

Full preparation details are in our Resources & Guides section.

[04] Professional vs DIY

What makes professional treatment different from a hardware-store spray.

Consumer bed bug sprays kill on contact then break down. Professional residual chemistry stays active on treated surfaces for weeks, reaching insects that return to harbourage points long after the technician leaves.

Residual chemistry

Professional-grade insecticide remains active on treated surfaces for weeks. Insects that contact treated zones days after the visit are still eliminated.

Precision application

Surface sprays miss the cracks and cavities where most bed bugs live. Our technicians apply product directly into seams, frame joints and headboard gaps where insects harbour.

Written treatment record

Every visit produces a typed report: which rooms were treated, which product was used, the re-entry time and what to watch for. Useful for tenants, landlords and strata managers.

Guaranteed follow-up

Bed bug eggs survive the first treatment. The follow-up visit inside the guarantee window catches the next generation — it's standard, not an optional extra.

[05] FAQ

Common questions about bed bug treatment in Canberra.

If your question isn't covered here, call us on 02 6105 9771 or send a message — we'll confirm the pest and treatment approach before we quote.

How many treatment visits will I need?
Most infestations clear with two visits: the initial treatment followed by a follow-up check inside the guarantee window, typically two to three weeks later. The follow-up is included in the quote — not billed separately. In severe or multi-room infestations, a third visit is occasionally needed and would be discussed after the initial inspection.
Do I have to throw out the mattress?
Rarely. In almost all cases, targeted treatment of the mattress seams and base is sufficient. Disposal is often counterproductive — it risks spreading bed bugs through common areas and leaves the bed frame untreated, which is equally important. We only recommend replacement if the mattress is structurally damaged or otherwise beyond use.
Is the treatment safe around children and pets?
Yes. We use the lowest-toxicity residual product effective against bed bugs. The key requirement is observing the re-entry time on your quote — typically two to four hours after treated areas have dried. Children and pets can return to the property after this period. Your technician confirms the exact re-entry time on the day.
How quickly can you come out?
We have same-week availability across Canberra and Queanbeyan. After receiving your enquiry, we respond within one business day with a written quote. The first treatment visit is usually scheduled within three to five days of quote approval.
Will I need to leave the property during treatment?
Yes — you and other occupants (including pets) need to vacate for the re-entry period, typically two to four hours. Fish tanks should be covered and air pumps switched off during treatment. Your written quote specifies the exact re-entry time for the products used.
Can bed bugs come back after treatment?
New introductions are always possible — bed bugs travel on luggage, second-hand furniture and with guests. If live activity returns after the guarantee window closes, it typically indicates a new introduction rather than a treatment failure. The initial visit and follow-up together are very effective at clearing an existing infestation. We include re-introduction prevention advice as part of every treatment.
Will a spray from a hardware store fix the problem?
Consumer sprays kill on contact but have no residual effect once dry. They also can't reach the inside of mattress seams, bed frame joints or headboard cavities where most bed bugs live. In most cases, a hardware-store spray delays professional treatment while the infestation grows.
What is the difference between "bed bug control" and "bed bug treatment"?
"Bed bug control" describes the broader management strategy — identification, prevention advice and ongoing monitoring. "Bed bug treatment" refers specifically to the chemical intervention that eliminates an active infestation. At TCB, our service covers both: we inspect, identify, treat all active harbourage zones with residual chemistry, and follow up inside the guarantee window.
